📊 BigBasket Data Breach (2020)

A Cybersecurity Awareness Guide

🔍 What Happened in the BigBasket Breach?

In October–November 2020, online grocery platform BigBasket experienced a major data breach when a large portion of its user database was accessed by unauthorized actors and put on the dark web for sale. The breach affected around 20 million (2 crore) users. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

📂 What Data Was Compromised?

According to security researchers monitoring the dark web:

  • Names and email addresses
  • Phone numbers
  • Hashed passwords (with possible weak hashing)
  • PINs and login IP addresses
  • Delivery addresses, dates of birth and location data :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

BigBasket stated that it does not store financial data such as credit card or debit card numbers; this data remained secure per company claims. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

⚠️ Why This Breach Matters

A breach of personal information like this increases cybersecurity risks such as:

  • Targeted phishing or scam attempts
  • Account takeover if reused passwords exist
  • Spam calls and unwanted contact
  • Data sold on underground marketplaces

🛡️ How Users Can Protect Themselves

  • ✔ Change passwords on platforms affected by breaches
  • ✔ Use unique passwords for every service
  • ✔ Enable two-factor authentication
  • ✔ Monitor email and banking for unusual activity
  • ✔ Consider a password manager to create and store secure passwords

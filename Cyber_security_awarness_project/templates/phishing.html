Interactive Cybersecurity Warning Signs

1. Suspicious Emails


Suspicious emails are fake messages designed to trick users into sharing personal or financial information.

  • Unknown or fake sender address
  • Spelling and grammar mistakes
  • Requests for passwords or bank details
  • Suspicious links or attachments
Tip: Never click unknown links.

2. Fake Website URLs

Fake websites look real but are created to steal your login credentials and personal data.

  • Misspelled website addresses
  • No HTTPS security lock
  • Poor design or broken pages
  • Forms asking for sensitive information
Tip: Always check the URL carefully.

3. Urgent Payment Requests

Scammers create urgency to force quick payments without verification.

  • Threats of account suspension
  • Requests for gift cards or cryptocurrency
  • Unexpected invoices or fines
  • Pressure to pay immediately
Tip: Legitimate companies do not rush payments.

4. Unexpected Login Alerts

Unexpected login alerts may indicate unauthorized access attempts.

  • Login alerts from unknown locations
  • Password reset emails you didn’t request
  • Multiple failed login attempts
  • Alerts from unfamiliar devices
Tip: Change your password immediately.

📱 Do Not Share OTPs

Protect Your Accounts from Fraud and Scams

1️⃣ What is an OTP?

OTP (One-Time Password) is a temporary security code sent to your mobile number or email to verify your identity during login, payments, or account recovery.

2️⃣ Why You Should Never Share OTPs

Fraudsters may pretend to be:

  • Bank officials
  • Customer care agents
  • Delivery executives
  • Lottery or prize organizers

If you share your OTP, attackers can:

  • Access your bank account
  • Transfer money
  • Reset your passwords
  • Take over your social media accounts

3️⃣ Safety Tips to Protect Your OTP

  • ✔ Never share OTP with anyone (even bank staff)
  • ✔ Banks NEVER ask for OTP over phone
  • ✔ Do not click suspicious links
  • ✔ Enable transaction alerts
  • ✔ Report fraud immediately

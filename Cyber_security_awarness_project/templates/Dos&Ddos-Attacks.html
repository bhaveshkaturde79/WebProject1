DoS & DDoS Attacks Awareness

Understanding Network Flooding Attacks

What is a DoS Attack?

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack occurs when a single system floods a server with traffic, making it unavailable to legitimate users.

What is a DDoS Attack?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack uses multiple infected systems (botnets) to overwhelm a target server with massive traffic.

Difference Between DoS and DDoS

  • DoS: Attack from a single system
  • DDoS: Attack from multiple systems
  • DDoS: Harder to detect and stop

How to Prevent DoS & DDoS Attacks

  • Use firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Enable rate limiting
  • Use DDoS protection services
  • Monitor unusual traffic spikes
  • Use Content Delivery Networks (CDN)

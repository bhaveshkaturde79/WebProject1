📌 What is Two-Factor Authentication?

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is an extra layer of security used to protect online accounts. Instead of only using a password, 2FA requires a second verification method. This makes it much harder for hackers to access your account even if they know your password.

🔑 How 2FA Works

  1. You enter your username and password.
  2. The system asks for a second verification code.
  3. You receive a code via SMS, email, or authentication app.
  4. After entering the code, access is granted.

This ensures that even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without the second factor.

🔐 Types of Two-Factor Authentication

  • SMS OTP: One-time password sent to your mobile phone.
  • Authentication Apps: Apps like Google Authenticator generate secure codes.
  • Email Verification: Code sent to registered email.
  • Biometric Verification: Fingerprint or face recognition.
  • Hardware Tokens: Physical security key devices.

🛡 Benefits of Enabling 2FA

  • Protects against password theft.
  • Reduces risk of phishing attacks.
  • Secures banking and social media accounts.
  • Prevents unauthorized access.
  • Improves overall cybersecurity.

💻 How to Enable 2FA

  1. Go to your account security settings.
  2. Find "Two-Factor Authentication" or "Security Settings."
  3. Choose your preferred verification method.
  4. Verify your phone number or install an authentication app.
  5. Enter the verification code.
  6. Save backup recovery codes.

🚨 What Happens Without 2FA?

Without 2FA, if your password is leaked or guessed, hackers can easily access your accounts. Cybercriminals often use phishing and data breaches to steal passwords. Enabling 2FA significantly reduces this risk.

📘 What is HTTP?

HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol) is the foundation of data communication on the web. It is used to transfer web pages from a server to your browser. When you type a website starting with http://, it means the data is transferred without encryption.

Because HTTP does not encrypt data, hackers can intercept sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal details.

🔐 HTTP vs HTTPS

  • HTTP: Data is not encrypted. Less secure.
  • HTTPS: Data is encrypted using SSL/TLS. More secure.
  • HTTPS protects users from eavesdropping and data tampering.
  • Always check for the padlock icon in the browser.

⚠ Why You Should Not Click Unknown Links

  • Fake banking websites
  • Phishing login pages
  • Malware downloads
  • Ransomware attacks
  • Identity theft

🌍 Real World Cyber Attack Examples

Many major cyber attacks start with a simple phishing email. Attackers disguise links to look like trusted companies such as banks, delivery services, or social media platforms.

Once clicked, the victim may unknowingly provide confidential information. Businesses lose billions of dollars every year due to phishing and online scams.

🛡 How to Protect Yourself

  • Always verify the website URL.
  • Look for HTTPS instead of HTTP.
  • Never click suspicious email links.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Install antivirus software.
  • Keep your browser and system updated.

🚨 What To Do If You Clicked a Suspicious Link

  1. Disconnect from the internet immediately.
  2. Run a full antivirus scan.
  3. Change your passwords.
  4. Inform your bank if financial details were entered.
  5. Report the phishing attempt.

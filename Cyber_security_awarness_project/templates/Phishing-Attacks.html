What is a Phishing Attack?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, or OTPs. These attacks usually occur through fake emails, messages, or websites that look like they come from trusted sources.

How Phishing Attacks Work

In a phishing attack, the attacker sends a fraudulent message that appears to be from a legitimate organization. The message contains a malicious link or attachment. Once the user clicks the link and enters their information, the attacker steals it.

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be from trusted companies asking for sensitive information.

Spear Phishing

Targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific individual or organization.

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Fraudulent text messages designed to trick users into clicking malicious links.

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Scam phone calls pretending to be from banks or government agencies.

Clone Phishing

A legitimate email is copied and resent with malicious links or attachments.

Signs of a Phishing Attack

  • Urgent or threatening messages
  • Suspicious email addresses or phone numbers
  • Spelling and grammatical mistakes
  • Unknown links or attachments
  • Requests for personal or financial information

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing

  • Never click on unknown or suspicious links
  • Verify sender email addresses carefully
  • Do not share OTPs or passwords with anyone
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
  • Use updated antivirus software

Real-Life Example

A user receives an email claiming their bank account will be blocked. The email asks them to click a link and verify details. The link leads to a fake website that steals login credentials.

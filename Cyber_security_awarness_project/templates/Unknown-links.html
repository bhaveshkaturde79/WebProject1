📌 Introduction to Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. One of the most common attack methods is malicious links sent via email, SMS, or social media. Attackers use psychological manipulation (social engineering) to trick users into clicking.

⚠ Types of Link-Based Threats

1. Phishing Attacks

Fake emails pretending to be from banks, delivery services, or social media platforms. They redirect you to fake login pages to steal credentials.

2. Malware Distribution

Clicking a link may automatically download malicious software like viruses, spyware, trojans, or ransomware.

3. Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts your files and demands payment to restore access. Many ransomware attacks begin with malicious email links.

4. Identity Theft

Attackers collect personal data such as passwords, credit card numbers, and government IDs for fraud.

🌍 Real-World Examples

  • Email pretending to be from a bank asking to "verify your account."
  • SMS claiming you won a lottery prize.
  • Fake courier message asking to track a package.
  • Social media message with “shocking video” link.

According to cybersecurity research, phishing remains one of the top causes of data breaches worldwide. Businesses lose billions of dollars annually due to phishing and ransomware attacks.

🛡 How to Protect Yourself

  • Check the sender's email carefully.
  • Hover over links to preview the real URL.
  • Use antivirus and firewall protection.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Keep your operating system updated.
  • Never enter credentials on suspicious websites.

🚨 What To Do If You Clicked a Malicious Link

  1. Disconnect from the internet immediately.
  2. Run a full system antivirus scan.
  3. Change all passwords from a secure device.
  4. Inform your bank if financial data was entered.
  5. Report the phishing attempt to cybersecurity authorities.
